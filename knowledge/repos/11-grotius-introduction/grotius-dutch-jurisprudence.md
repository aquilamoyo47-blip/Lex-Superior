# Hugo Grotius — Introduction to Dutch Jurisprudence
## Inleidinge tot de Hollandsche Rechtsgeleerdheid (1631)
## Public Domain — Foundational Roman-Dutch Law Text
## Adapted for Zimbabwe Superior Courts

### Book I: On Persons and Their Rights

**On Natural Liberty**

All persons, by natural law, are born free and equal. This principle of natural liberty forms the foundation of all human rights jurisprudence. It was this principle which the Roman-Dutch lawyers inherited from Roman law and which permeates the common law of Zimbabwe through the colonial inheritance of Roman-Dutch law.

**On the Status of Persons**

Persons in law are either natural or juristic. A natural person acquires legal personality at birth and loses it at death. A juristic person (corporate entity) acquires personality upon registration and its rights and obligations are separate from those of its members.

### Book II: On Things and Rights thereto

**Division of Things**

Things are either in commerce (res in commercio) or outside commerce (res extra commercium). Things which are the subject of commerce may be owned privately; things outside commerce include the sea, the air, and running water.

**On Ownership**

Ownership is the most complete right a person can have in a thing. It includes the rights to use, to enjoy the fruits, and to dispose of the thing. Ownership may be limited by servitudes and other real rights.

**On Real Rights in Immovable Property**

In Roman-Dutch law, real rights in immovable property include—
(i) ownership;
(ii) servitudes (praedial and personal);
(iii) mortgage (hypothec);
(iv) usufruct;
(v) real security rights.

In Zimbabwe, real rights in immovable property must be registered in the Deeds Registry to be enforceable against third parties, per the Deeds Registries Act [Chapter 20:05].

**On Mortgage (Hypothec)**

A hypothec (mortgage bond) is a real security right over immovable property. It does not involve transfer of possession. The creditor has the right to cause the property to be sold in execution if the debt is not paid.

A mortgage bond in Zimbabwe is registered in the Deeds Registry and ranks according to the date of registration. A prior registered bond has priority over a subsequent registered bond.

### Book III: On Obligations

**Sources of Obligations**

Grotius recognised four sources of obligation:
(i) Law (ex lege);
(ii) Agreement (ex conventione);
(iii) Wrongful act (ex maleficio); and
(iv) Juristic act other than agreement.

**On Agreement**

A valid agreement requires—
(a) capacity of the parties;
(b) consensus (meeting of minds);
(c) lawfulness (the object must not be contra bonos mores or prohibited by law);
(d) possibility (the performance must be possible).

**On Breach of Contract**

A party is in breach of contract if—
(a) the party fails to perform when performance is due (mora debitoris);
(b) the party performs defectively;
(c) the party makes performance impossible; or
(d) in a reciprocal contract, the party repudiates the contract before performance is due (anticipatory breach).

**Mora Debitoris and Mora Creditoris**

Mora debitoris (debtor's delay) occurs when a debtor fails to perform timeously. Where time is of the essence, mere expiry of the time period puts the debtor in mora automatically (mora ex re). In other cases, a demand (interpellatio) is required (mora ex persona).

Mora creditoris (creditor's delay) occurs when the creditor wrongfully fails to accept the debtor's tender of performance.

### Book IV: On Succession

**Testate and Intestate Succession**

Succession is either testate (by will) or intestate (in terms of the law where no valid will exists). In Zimbabwe, intestate succession is governed by the Administration of Estates Act [Chapter 6:01] and the Customary Law (Application Act) [Chapter 7:05].

**On Wills**

A valid will requires—
(a) testamentary capacity;
(b) the proper formalities;
(c) freedom from undue influence.

The formalities for a will in Zimbabwe are prescribed by the Wills Act [Chapter 6:06].
