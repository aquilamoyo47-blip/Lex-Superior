# Van der Linden — Institutes of Holland
## Joannes van der Linden — Rechtsgeleerd Practicaal en Koopmans Handboek (1806)
## Public Domain — Roman-Dutch Law Text
## Adapted for Zimbabwe Superior Courts

### On the Law of Contract

**General Principles**

A contract is a bilateral legal act by which two or more persons are bound to one another by an obligation. Van der Linden, following Grotius and Voet, held that the foundation of all contractual obligation is the free will of the contracting parties — the autonomous agreement of persons of full legal capacity.

**Requirements for a Valid Contract**
1. **Capacity:** The parties must have the capacity to contract. Minors and persons of unsound mind have limited or no capacity.
2. **Consensus:** There must be a genuine meeting of minds — not mere outward agreement. Error, misrepresentation, and duress vitiate consent.
3. **Legality:** The contract must not be contrary to law or contra bonos mores.
4. **Possibility:** The obligation must be capable of performance.

**Performance and Breach**

Van der Linden emphasised that the creditor has the right to demand specific performance of the obligation. Only where specific performance is impossible or would cause disproportionate hardship does the obligation transform into one of damages.

This principle — the preference for specific performance over damages — persists in Zimbabwe's common law. See *Farmers' Co-operative Society (Regt.) v Barry* 1912 AD 343, which remains good law in Zimbabwe.

### On the Law of Delict

**Aquilian Action**

The Lex Aquilia gave rise to the primary delictual remedy in Roman-Dutch law. Van der Linden's formulation of the requirements for Aquilian liability corresponds to the modern requirements in Zimbabwe:

(i) **Act or omission** — conduct by the defendant;
(ii) **Wrongfulness** — the conduct is unlawful (contra ius);
(iii) **Fault** — dolus (intention) or culpa (negligence);
(iv) **Causation** — the conduct caused the harm;
(v) **Harm** — the plaintiff suffered patrimonial loss.

**Actio iniuriarum**

The actio iniuriarum protects personality rights — the dignity, good name, and bodily integrity of the person. Unlike Aquilian liability, the actio iniuriarum does not require proof of patrimonial loss; it compensates for the insult to personality.

In Zimbabwe, defamation and related claims are governed by the actio iniuriarum principles.

### On the Law of Property

**Transfer of Ownership**

Van der Linden followed the principle of Roman law that transfer of ownership of movable property requires—
(a) **Agreement** (iusta causa) — a valid underlying reason for the transfer; and
(b) **Delivery** (traditio) — the actual transfer of possession.

For immovable property in Zimbabwe, registration in the Deeds Registry is required in place of traditio (Deeds Registries Act [Chapter 20:05]). Registration is constitutive; the property rights pass upon registration, not upon signing of the transfer deed.

**Ownership versus Possession**

Ownership (dominium) is the most complete real right. Possession (possessio) is the physical control of a thing with the intention to hold it as owner. Possession is protected by possessory interdicts, regardless of the underlying right to own.

The distinction is fundamental in Zimbabwe law: even a thief who is in possession may invoke the mandament van spolie to recover possession from someone who takes the property from him unlawfully.

### On Commercial Law

**Bills of Exchange**

Van der Linden's treatment of negotiable instruments reflects the civil law of obligations adapted for commercial use. The strict formalities of negotiable instruments (bills of exchange, promissory notes, cheques) were designed to give certainty in commercial transactions.

In Zimbabwe, bills of exchange are governed by the Bills of Exchange Act [Chapter 14:02], which follows the English Bills of Exchange Act 1882 pattern.

**Partnership**

A partnership in Roman-Dutch law arises from the agreement of two or more persons to pool contributions and share in the profits and losses of a common enterprise. Each partner is an agent of the partnership and may bind the partnership by acts done within the scope of the partnership business.

A partner who has knowledge of, or acquiesces in, another partner's unauthorized acts may be bound by those acts under the doctrine of apparent authority.

### On Procedure

**Van der Linden on the Role of the Advocate**

Van der Linden emphasised that the advocate's primary duty is to the court, and secondarily to the client. The advocate must not knowingly mislead the court, must cite adverse authorities, and must maintain the dignity of the court.

This principle is codified in Zimbabwe in the Legal Practitioners Code of Conduct, SI 37 of 2018, which requires legal practitioners to—
(a) act with integrity and in a manner consistent with the dignity of the legal profession;
(b) uphold the due administration of justice;
(c) not deceive or knowingly mislead the court;
(d) cite any authority that is adverse to the client's interest which has not been cited by the opponent.
