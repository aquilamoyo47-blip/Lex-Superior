# Supreme Court Rules, SI 84 of 2018
## Zimbabwe — Supreme Court Rules 2018
## Statutory Instrument 84 of 2018

### Part 1: Preliminary

**Rule 1: Citation**
These rules may be cited as the Supreme Court Rules, 2018.

**Rule 2: Definitions**
In these rules—
"appellant" means a person who notes an appeal to the Supreme Court;
"bench" means a panel of Judges constituted to hear an appeal;
"grounds of appeal" means the grounds upon which an appellant relies on an appeal;
"registrar" means the Registrar of the Supreme Court;
"respondent" means the person against whom an appeal is noted.

### Part 2: Noting Appeals

**Rule 5: Noting an appeal**
(1) An appeal to the Supreme Court from the High Court in a civil matter shall be noted within 15 days of the date of judgment appealed against.
(2) An appeal shall be noted by filing—
(a) a notice of appeal; and
(b) the prescribed fee.
(3) The notice of appeal shall set out—
(a) the names of the parties;
(b) the judgment appealed against;
(c) the specific grounds of appeal;
(d) the order sought on appeal.

**Rule 6: Grounds of appeal**
(1) The grounds of appeal shall be clear and concise.
(2) The grounds of appeal shall identify whether the ground is one of law or one of fact, or both.
(3) Grounds of appeal that are vague or general will not be entertained.

**Rule 7: Leave to appeal**
(1) An appeal against an interlocutory order of the High Court requires leave of the Supreme Court.
(2) An application for leave to appeal shall be made within 15 days of the interlocutory order.

### Part 3: Record on Appeal

**Rule 10: Preparation of record**
(1) After noting an appeal, the appellant shall take all necessary steps to procure the preparation and transmission of the record to the Supreme Court.
(2) The record shall contain—
(a) the notice of appeal;
(b) all pleadings;
(c) all orders and judgments;
(d) the transcript of the evidence.

**Rule 11: Filing of record**
(1) The record shall be filed with the registrar within 3 months of the date the appeal was noted.
(2) Failure to file the record within the prescribed period may result in the appeal being deemed abandoned.

### Part 4: Heads of Argument

**Rule 15: Appellant's heads of argument**
(1) The appellant shall file heads of argument within 20 days of filing the record.
(2) The heads of argument shall—
(a) set out the issues on appeal;
(b) state the legal propositions relied upon;
(c) cite relevant authorities;
(d) be concise and well-organized.

**Rule 16: Respondent's heads of argument**
(1) The respondent shall file heads of argument within 20 days of being served with the appellant's heads of argument.
(2) The respondent's heads shall respond to each ground of appeal.

### Part 5: Hearing of Appeals

**Rule 20: Date of hearing**
(1) After the filing of heads of argument, the registrar shall allocate a date for hearing.
(2) The registrar shall notify the parties of the date of hearing at least 21 days in advance.

**Rule 21: Conduct of hearing**
(1) At the hearing of an appeal, the appellant's counsel shall address the court first.
(2) The respondent's counsel shall then address the court.
(3) The appellant's counsel may reply briefly.
(4) The court may, at any time, ask questions of counsel.

**Rule 22: Judgment**
(1) The court may give judgment at the conclusion of the hearing or at a later date.
(2) Judgment shall be delivered in writing.

### Part 6: Powers of Supreme Court on Appeal

**Rule 25: Powers of court on appeal**
On appeal, the Supreme Court may—
(a) confirm, vary or set aside the judgment appealed against;
(b) remit the matter to the High Court for a re-hearing;
(c) substitute any judgment it thinks fit;
(d) make such order as to costs as it thinks fit.

**Rule 26: Finality of Supreme Court orders**
A judgment or order of the Supreme Court is final and binding, subject only to the jurisdiction of the Constitutional Court.
