# High Court of Zimbabwe — Practice Directions
## Consolidated Practice Directions for Civil Proceedings

### Practice Direction 1: Case Management

**1.1 Pre-trial Conferences**
All defended civil actions shall be subject to mandatory pre-trial conference under Rule 111 of the High Court Rules. The pre-trial conference shall be conducted before a judge or registrar. Parties must attend in person or be represented by counsel with authority to settle.

**1.2 Case Management Orders**
The court may at any stage issue case management orders including—
(a) orders for exchange of documents and expert reports;
(b) timetables for completion of interlocutory steps;
(c) directions for trial preparation;
(d) orders for discovery.

### Practice Direction 2: Urgent Applications

**2.1 Certification of Urgency**
Every urgent application shall be accompanied by a certificate of urgency signed by counsel. The certificate shall set out—
(a) the nature and extent of the urgency;
(b) why the matter cannot be heard in the ordinary course;
(c) what irreparable harm will result from delay.

**2.2 Time Requirements**
(a) An urgent application where notice is given: minimum 24 hours' notice to respondent.
(b) An urgent application without notice (ex parte): strictly reserved for cases where notice will defeat the purpose of the order or where delay will cause irreparable harm.
(c) Ex parte orders are provisional and must be served immediately upon the respondent with a return date.

**2.3 Duty Judge**
Applications where notice cannot be given shall be placed before the duty judge. The duty judge rotates weekly among the judges of the High Court.

### Practice Direction 3: Electronic Filing

**3.1 Electronic submission**
The High Court accepts electronic filing through the approved court management system. Hard copies must be filed simultaneously for all affidavits, pleadings, and heads of argument.

**3.2 Format requirements**
Documents filed electronically shall be—
(a) in PDF format;
(b) searchable (not scanned images);
(c) properly paginated;
(d) not exceed 10MB per document.

### Practice Direction 4: Heads of Argument

**4.1 Format of heads of argument**
Heads of argument filed in the High Court shall—
(a) not exceed 30 pages without leave of court;
(b) be divided into numbered paragraphs;
(c) set out the facts, issues and legal submissions in a logical sequence;
(d) include a table of cases, statutes, and other authorities cited;
(e) append copies of any unreported judgments cited.

**4.2 Citation of authorities**
(a) Zimbabwe cases shall be cited first.
(b) South African authorities shall be cited as persuasive.
(c) English authorities may be cited where relevant to Roman-Dutch law principles.
(d) SADC Tribunal decisions may be cited for comparative purposes.

**4.3 Unreported judgments**
Where an unreported judgment is cited, a copy shall be attached to the heads of argument and provided to the opposing party.

### Practice Direction 5: Judgment Debts and Execution

**5.1 Writ of execution — movables**
(1) Before executing against immovable property, execution must first be levied against movable property.
(2) The judgment creditor shall first obtain a writ of execution against movables.
(3) If movables are insufficient, an application to declare immovable property specially executable shall be made.

**5.2 Declaring immovable property executable**
(1) An application to declare immovable property specially executable shall be on notice to—
(a) the judgment debtor;
(b) any registered mortgagee;
(c) any other person with a registered real right in the property.
(2) The court shall consider the proportionality of the execution against the amount of the judgment debt.

**5.3 Emoluments attachment orders**
(1) An emoluments attachment order shall be served on the employer.
(2) The employer shall deduct the specified amount from each payment of salary or wages.
(3) The maximum deductible is one-third of the judgment debtor's salary.

### Practice Direction 6: Service Out of Jurisdiction

**6.1 Service outside Zimbabwe**
(1) Leave of court is required to serve process outside Zimbabwe.
(2) The application for leave shall show that—
(a) the High Court has jurisdiction;
(b) the defendant is a necessary or proper party to the proceedings;
(c) there is a serious question to be tried.

**6.2 Service within Africa**
Service on defendants within SADC member states shall be effected through the mechanism established by the Civil Matters (Mutual Assistance) Act [Chapter 8:02].

### Practice Direction 7: Expert Evidence

**7.1 Expert reports**
(1) A party intending to call an expert witness shall deliver a summary of the expert's evidence at least 15 days before trial.
(2) The summary shall set out the expert's qualifications and the substance of the opinion to be given.

**7.2 Joint experts**
The court may order that expert evidence be given by a single joint expert appointed by agreement of the parties or by the court.

### Practice Direction 8: Constitutional Matters

**8.1 Referral to Constitutional Court**
(1) Where the validity of a law is challenged, the matter shall be referred to the Constitutional Court.
(2) The High Court may not determine a constitutional matter relating to the validity of an Act of Parliament.

**8.2 Notice to Attorney General**
(1) Where the constitutionality of a law is raised, the Attorney General shall be given notice of the proceedings.
(2) The Attorney General may intervene as amicus curiae.

### Practice Direction 9: Consent Papers and Settlements

**9.1 Consent orders**
(1) Parties may settle a matter by submitting consent papers to the registrar.
(2) Consent papers shall be signed by both parties or their legal representatives.
(3) The court shall make an order in terms of the consent papers.

**9.2 Recording of settlement**
The settlement agreement shall be filed with the registrar and may be made an order of court.
