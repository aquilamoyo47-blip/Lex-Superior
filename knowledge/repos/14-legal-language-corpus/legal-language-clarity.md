# Legal Language Clarity — Drafting Resources
## Clear Legal Writing for Zimbabwe Superior Courts

### Principles of Plain Legal Language

**1. Shorter Sentences**
Long sentences increase cognitive load and risk ambiguity. The recommended maximum sentence length for legal documents is 25-30 words. Complex conditions should be structured as numbered sub-clauses.

Example of poor drafting:
"The defendant shall, within a period of not less than ten (10) days and not more than fifteen (15) days following the date on which the plaintiff's notice of set-down is served upon the defendant or the defendant's legal representative at the defendant's address for service of process, file and serve upon the plaintiff's attorneys a notice of intention to oppose the application."

Example of clear drafting:
"The defendant shall file a notice of opposition within 10 to 15 days of being served with the notice of set-down."

**2. Active Voice**
Active voice is clearer than passive voice. The subject of the sentence should be the actor.

Passive: "The summons shall be served on the defendant by the sheriff."
Active: "The sheriff shall serve the summons on the defendant."

**3. Consistent Terminology**
Use the same term consistently. Do not use "agreement," "contract," and "arrangement" interchangeably if they all refer to the same document.

**4. Defined Terms**
Define complex or technical terms at the outset of the document. Use the defined term consistently thereafter.

### Structure of Legal Arguments

**The IRAC Method**

Heads of argument and legal opinions should follow the IRAC structure:
- **Issue** — state the precise legal issue
- **Rule** — state the applicable legal rule (statute, common law, case law)
- **Application** — apply the rule to the specific facts
- **Conclusion** — state the result of the application

**Example:**

*Issue:* Whether the plaintiff's claim has prescribed.

*Rule:* Section 11(d) of the Prescription Act [Chapter 8:11] provides that debts are extinguished by prescription after three years. Section 12(1) provides that prescription commences to run as soon as the debt is due.

*Application:* The plaintiff's debt became due on 1 January 2019. The summons was served on 15 February 2022, more than three years after the debt became due. No interruption of prescription is alleged.

*Conclusion:* The plaintiff's claim has prescribed and should be dismissed.

### Common Drafting Errors in Zimbabwe Legal Practice

**1. Failure to allege facts**
A declaration must allege facts, not conclusions. "The defendant was negligent" is a conclusion; "the defendant drove at 120km/h in a 60km/h zone" is a fact.

**2. Commingling of facts and law**
Pleadings should contain facts. Legal conclusions are for argument.

**3. Vague relief**
The prayer must be specific. "Such relief as the court deems fit" without more is insufficient.

**4. Failure to allege jurisdiction**
In every pleading, it should appear that the court has jurisdiction. If jurisdiction is not apparent, it must be expressly alleged.

**5. Incorrect citation**
Cases must be correctly cited. Zimbabwe Law Reports citation format: *Party v Party* YEAR (Volume) ZLR PAGE (Court abbreviation). E.g., *Garber v Witwatersrand Bank* 2001 (2) ZLR 14 (H).

### Legal Writing in the Superior Courts — Tone and Register

**Tone**
Legal writing in Zimbabwe's Superior Courts must be—
(a) Formal but not archaic;
(b) Assertive but respectful;
(c) Precise without being pedantic;
(d) Concise without sacrificing accuracy.

**Advocacy Language**
"It is respectfully submitted that..." (correct)
"I argue that..." (less formal; acceptable in written submissions but prefer "I submit")
"It is clear that..." (use sparingly — only when truly clear)
"Obviously..." (avoid — if something is obvious, a court will see it; asserting it is obvious invites the court to find it is not)

**Addressing the Court**
- High Court: "Your Lordship" / "My Lord" / "My Lady"
- Supreme Court: "Your Lordships" / "My Lords" / "My Ladies"
- Constitutional Court: "Your Lordships" / "Honourable Justices"
- Magistrates Court: "Your Worship"

### Citation of Authorities

**Zimbabwe Cases (Primary Authority)**
*Surname v Surname* YEAR (Vol) ZLR PAGE (Court)

Court abbreviations:
- H = High Court
- S = Supreme Court
- CC = Constitutional Court
- SC = Supreme Court (older format)

**South African Cases (Persuasive Authority)**
*Surname v Surname* YEAR (Vol) SA PAGE (Court)

Court abbreviations (SA):
- A = Appellate Division (now SCA)
- CC = Constitutional Court
- SCA = Supreme Court of Appeal

**Unreported Cases**
Where an unreported judgment is cited, the case number format is used:
HH 234/2019 (Harare High Court, case 234 of 2019)
SC 45/2020 (Supreme Court, case 45 of 2020)

**Statutes**
Full citation: *High Court Act [Chapter 7:06]*
Short citation: *the Act*
Statutory Instrument: *SI 202 of 2021*

**Secondary Sources**
Author, *Title* (edition) page.
E.g., *Herbstein & Van Winsen, Civil Practice of the High Courts of South Africa* (5th ed) 456.
