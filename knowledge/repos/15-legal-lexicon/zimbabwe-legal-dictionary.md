# Zimbabwe Legal Lexicon — Legal Dictionary and Glossary
## Terms of Art, Latin Maxims, and Court Terminology

### Civil Procedure Terminology

**Ab initio** — from the beginning. A contract void ab initio is void from its inception.

**Absente reo** — in the absence of the accused/defendant.

**Actio** — an action; a legal proceeding. Roman law recognised different actiones for different causes.

**Actio confessoria** — an action to enforce a servitude.

**Actio in personam** — an action against a person (as distinct from an action against a thing).

**Actio in rem** — an action against a thing; a possessory or ownership action.

**Actio negatoria** — an action denying the existence of a servitude.

**Audi alteram partem** — hear the other side. A fundamental principle of natural justice requiring that no person be condemned without being given an opportunity to be heard. Entrenched in section 68 of the Constitution of Zimbabwe 2013.

**Bona fide** — in good faith; genuinely. A bona fide defence is one which is genuine, not fictitious.

**Certiorari** — let it be certified. A prerogative order by which a superior court quashes the decision of an inferior court or tribunal on grounds of excess of jurisdiction or irregularity.

**Cession** — the transfer of a personal right from one person (the cedent) to another (the cessionary). In Zimbabwe, governed by the common law. Cession is effected by agreement and does not require the consent of the debtor.

**Condictio** — a personal action for unjustified enrichment under Roman-Dutch law.

**Condictio causa data causa non secuta** — a condictio for recovery of money paid in advance where the purpose for which payment was made has not materialised.

**Condictio indebiti** — an action for recovery of money paid in the mistaken belief that it was owed.

**Condictio ob turpem vel iniustam causam** — a condictio where the payment was made for an illegal or immoral purpose.

**Contra bonos mores** — contrary to good morals; contrary to public policy. A contract contra bonos mores is void.

**Curator ad litem** — a person appointed by the court to represent the interests of a litigant who lacks legal capacity (e.g., a minor, or person of unsound mind) in specific litigation.

**Curator bonis** — a person appointed by the court to manage the property of an incapacitated person.

**Custodia legis** — in the custody of the law. Property in custodia legis is in the possession of an officer of the court.

**Declaratory order** — an order of court declaring the rights of the parties without granting consequential relief. Available under section 14 of the High Court Act [Chapter 7:06].

**Defamation** — the wrongful, intentional publication of a defamatory statement about a person. In Zimbabwe, governed by the common law.

**Default judgment** — judgment entered against a defendant who has failed to defend or who has failed to appear at trial.

**Delectus personae** — the selection of a specific person. In contracts of delectus personae (e.g., employment, partnership), the parties contract with specific persons in mind and the contract cannot be ceded or delegated without consent.

**Delivery** — the transfer of possession. In Roman-Dutch law, delivery (traditio) is required for the transfer of ownership of movable property.

**Dolus** — deceit; intention. In delict, dolus is the intentional commission of a wrongful act.

**Dominium** — ownership; the real right of full ownership over property.

**Donatio** — a gift. In Roman-Dutch law, a donation is gratuitous — there is no counter-performance by the donee.

**Duplum rule** — the common law rule that contractual interest may not exceed the capital amount of the debt. Codified in the Money Lending and Rates of Interest Act [Chapter 14:14].

**Ejectment** — the remedy for recovery of possession of land or premises from a person in unlawful occupation.

**Enrichment** — in law, the unjustified increase of one person's estate at the expense of another, giving rise to an obligation to make restitution.

**Exception** — a pleading objection to a declaration on grounds that it lacks averments necessary to sustain an action, or is vague and embarrassing.

**Exceptio non adimpleti contractus** — the defence that the plaintiff has not performed or tendered performance of their own obligation in a reciprocal contract.

**Ex parte** — on behalf of one party only; without notice to the other party.

**Factum valet** — the act is valid. Used to describe cases where an act, though irregular, is nevertheless valid in law.

**Fiduciary** — a person who acts in a position of trust on behalf of another.

**Fides** — faith; trust. In Roman-Dutch law, bona fides permeates contractual relationships.

**Fungible** — interchangeable. Money is fungible; so is grain or oil of the same quality.

**Hypothec** — a real security right over property without transfer of possession. In Zimbabwe, includes the landlord's tacit hypothec over movable property of the tenant on the leased premises.

**In limine** — at the threshold; a preliminary point raised at the outset of proceedings.

**Incola** — a person domiciled in a jurisdiction. An incola is subject to the personal jurisdiction of the courts of that jurisdiction.

**Interdict** — a court order requiring a person to refrain from doing something (prohibitory interdict) or to do something (mandatory interdict).

**Interim interdict** — a temporary interdict, usually granted pending the determination of the main application.

**Iudex** — a judge.

**Judgment** — the final decision of a court in an action.

**Jurisdiction** — the power of a court to hear and determine a matter.

**Jus cogens** — peremptory norms of international law from which no derogation is permitted.

**Laches** — unreasonable delay in asserting one's rights, which may disentitle a party to equitable relief.

**Lex fori** — the law of the forum; the law of the court hearing the matter.

**Lex loci contractus** — the law of the place where the contract was made.

**Liquidated claim** — a claim for a specific sum of money or one capable of determination by arithmetic.

**Lis pendens** — a pending suit. A special plea of lis pendens is raised where the same matter between the same parties is pending in another court.

**Locus standi** — standing; the right to appear before a court. A person must have locus standi to bring or defend proceedings.

**Mandament van spolie** — a possessory interdict restoring possession that has been unlawfully disturbed, without inquiry into the underlying right to possession.

**Mandamus** — a prerogative order commanding a person or body to perform a public duty.

**Mora** — delay; default. Mora debitoris is the debtor's failure to perform timeously. Mora ex re arises automatically upon expiry of a time of the essence; mora ex persona requires a demand.

**Negligence (culpa)** — the failure to take the care that a reasonable person would take in the circumstances.

**Nemo dat quod non habet** — no one can give what they do not have. A seller cannot pass better title than they have.

**Numerus clausus** — the principle that real rights are limited to those recognised by law; parties cannot create new real rights by agreement.

**Onus** — burden of proof. The onus probandi lies on the party who asserts a positive fact.

**Order** — a court decision in an application.

**Pacta sunt servanda** — agreements must be kept. The fundamental principle that valid contracts are binding and must be performed.

**Peremption** — the abandonment of a right by conduct inconsistent with the intention to enforce it.

**Peregrinus** — a foreigner; a person not domiciled in the jurisdiction. A peregrinus may be subject to the jurisdiction of the court in respect of property situated in the jurisdiction.

**Pleadings** — the formal written statements by which the parties define the issues to be determined at trial.

**Prescription** — the extinction of a right or debt by the lapse of time. In Zimbabwe, governed by the Prescription Act [Chapter 8:11].

**Prohibition** — a prerogative order restraining an inferior court from exceeding its jurisdiction.

**Quid pro quo** — something for something; a counter-performance; consideration.

**Rei vindicatio** — the owner's action to recover property from any person in possession of it, without consent.

**Rescission** — the setting aside of a contract or judgment ab initio.

**Res judicata** — a matter already adjudicated. A final judgment on the merits by a competent court bars relitigation of the same matter between the same parties.

**Servitude** — a real right limiting the use of one piece of land for the benefit of another (praedial servitude) or for the benefit of a person (personal servitude such as usufruct).

**Set-off** — the right of a debtor to reduce the amount owed to a creditor by an amount which the creditor owes to the debtor. In Roman-Dutch law, set-off (compensatio) operates automatically when both debts are liquidated and payable.

**Sine die** — without a day; indefinitely.

**Specific performance** — the remedy compelling a party to perform their contractual obligation in specie (as contracted), as opposed to claiming damages.

**Spoliation** — the unlawful deprivation of possession.

**Summons** — the process by which a defendant is called upon to appear in court.

**Tacit term** — a term implied into a contract by law because of the nature of the contract and the presumed intention of the parties.

**Ultra vires** — beyond the powers. An act beyond the powers of the person or body performing it is ultra vires and void.

**Unliquidated claim** — a claim for damages which must be assessed by the court.

**Usufruct** — a personal servitude giving the holder the right to use and enjoy another's property, without destroying its substance.

**Void** — a nullity; of no legal effect from the outset.

**Voidable** — capable of being set aside at the election of one party, but valid unless and until set aside.

**Waiver** — the intentional relinquishment of a known right.

**Writ of execution** — process directing an officer of the court to enforce a judgment.
