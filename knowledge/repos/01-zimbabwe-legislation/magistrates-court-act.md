# Magistrates Court Act [Chapter 7:10]
## Zimbabwe Legislation — Primary Source

### Part I: Preliminary

**1. Short title**
This Act may be cited as the Magistrates Court Act [Chapter 7:10].

**2. Interpretation**
In this Act—
"civil jurisdiction" means the jurisdiction of the magistrates court in civil matters;
"magistrate" means a person appointed as a magistrate under this Act;
"regional magistrate" means a magistrate appointed to preside over a regional magistrates court.

### Part III: Civil Jurisdiction

**10. Civil jurisdiction of magistrates courts**
(1) A magistrates court shall have civil jurisdiction to hear and determine civil matters where the claim or value of the matter in dispute does not exceed the monetary limit prescribed from time to time by the Minister.
(2) The civil jurisdiction of a magistrates court extends to—
(a) actions for the recovery of money;
(b) actions for the recovery of movable property;
(c) actions for the delivery or transfer of movable property;
(d) actions for ejectment from or for the payment of rent in respect of immovable property.

**11. Limits of jurisdiction**
(1) A magistrates court shall not have jurisdiction in matters relating to—
(a) the status of a person;
(b) the dissolution of a marriage;
(c) insolvency or liquidation;
(d) the interpretation of a will or other testamentary document;
(e) matters involving an amount exceeding the prescribed limit.

**12. Concurrent jurisdiction**
The jurisdiction of the magistrates court in civil matters is concurrent with that of the High Court, except where exclusive jurisdiction is conferred by law.

### Part IV: Procedure

**15. Commencement of proceedings**
Civil proceedings in a magistrates court shall be commenced by the issue of a summons in the prescribed form.

**16. Service of summons**
(1) A summons shall be served personally on the defendant.
(2) Where personal service cannot be effected, the court may order substituted service by—
(a) affixing a copy to the outer door of the defendant's residence or place of business; or
(b) publication in a newspaper.

**17. Appearance to defend**
A defendant who wishes to defend a claim shall file a notice of appearance to defend within the period prescribed by the Rules.

**18. Plea**
A defendant who has entered an appearance to defend shall file a plea within the period prescribed by the Rules.

**19. Default judgment**
(1) If a defendant fails to enter an appearance to defend within the prescribed period, the plaintiff may apply for judgment by default.
(2) A default judgment may be set aside by the court on good cause shown.

**20. Summary judgment**
(1) A plaintiff may apply for summary judgment where the defendant has entered an appearance but the claim is for—
(a) a liquidated amount;
(b) the delivery of movable property;
(c) ejectment.
(2) The defendant may oppose summary judgment by filing an opposing affidavit disclosing a bona fide defence.

### Part V: Execution

**25. Writ of execution**
(1) A judgment of a magistrates court may be enforced by writ of execution.
(2) A writ of execution shall be issued by the registrar or clerk of the court.
(3) A writ of execution shall direct the messenger of court to attach and sell so much of the judgment debtor's movable property as is sufficient to satisfy the judgment.

**26. Emoluments attachment order**
A judgment creditor may apply to the court for an emoluments attachment order directing the judgment debtor's employer to deduct the judgment debt from the judgment debtor's salary or wages.
