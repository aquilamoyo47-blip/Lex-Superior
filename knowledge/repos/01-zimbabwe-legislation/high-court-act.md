# High Court Act [Chapter 7:06]
## Zimbabwe Legislation — Primary Source

### Part I: Preliminary

**1. Short title**
This Act may be cited as the High Court Act [Chapter 7:06].

**2. Interpretation**
In this Act—
"civil jurisdiction" means the jurisdiction of the High Court to hear and determine civil causes and matters;
"the Court" means the High Court of Zimbabwe established by section 3;
"Judge" means a Judge of the High Court;
"Judge President" means the Judge President of the High Court appointed under section 5.

### Part II: Establishment and Constitution of the High Court

**3. Establishment of High Court**
There is hereby established a court to be known as the High Court of Zimbabwe which shall be a superior court of record.

**4. Composition of High Court**
The High Court shall consist of—
(a) a Judge President; and
(b) such other Judges as may be appointed from time to time.

**5. Appointment of Judges**
(1) The Judge President and other Judges shall be appointed by the President in accordance with section 180 of the Constitution.
(2) A person shall not be qualified for appointment as a Judge unless he is qualified for appointment as a judge of the Supreme Court.

### Part III: Jurisdiction of the High Court

**13. Civil jurisdiction**
(1) The High Court shall have full original civil jurisdiction over all persons and over all matters within Zimbabwe.
(2) The civil jurisdiction of the High Court shall include jurisdiction in all matters of law and fact.

**14. Appellate jurisdiction**
The High Court shall have jurisdiction to hear and determine appeals from any inferior court in civil matters.

**15. Review jurisdiction**
The High Court shall have jurisdiction to review the proceedings and decisions of any inferior court, tribunal or administrative authority.

**16. Jurisdiction in matters of insolvency**
The High Court shall have jurisdiction to hear and determine all matters relating to insolvency.

**17. Jurisdiction in matrimonial matters**
The High Court shall have jurisdiction in all matrimonial matters including divorce, judicial separation, nullity of marriage, and all ancillary matters.

### Part IV: Procedure

**25. Rules of Court**
The Chief Justice may make Rules of Court for regulating and prescribing the procedure and practice to be followed in the High Court.

**26. Right of appearance**
(1) A party to any proceedings in the High Court may appear in person or be represented by a legal practitioner.
(2) A body corporate or unincorporate shall be represented by a legal practitioner unless the Court otherwise orders.

**27. Process of Court**
All process of the Court shall be issued in the name of the High Court of Zimbabwe.

**28. Service of process**
(1) Subject to this Act and the Rules of Court, all process of the Court shall be served personally on the defendant or respondent.
(2) Where personal service cannot be effected, the Court may order substituted service.

### Part V: Enforcement of Judgments

**35. Enforcement of civil judgments**
A judgment or order of the High Court in a civil matter shall be enforced in accordance with the Rules of Court.

**36. Contempt of court**
Any person who wilfully fails to comply with an order of the High Court shall be guilty of contempt of court and may be punished by the Court.

**37. Execution of judgments**
(1) A judgment creditor may apply for a writ of execution to enforce a money judgment.
(2) Execution may be levied against the movable or immovable property of the judgment debtor.

### Schedule: Fees

The fees payable in respect of proceedings in the High Court shall be as prescribed from time to time by the Rules Committee.
