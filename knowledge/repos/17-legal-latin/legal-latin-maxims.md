# Legal Latin Maxims — Comprehensive Glossary
## Zimbabwe Superior Courts — Latin Terms with Common Law Context

### A

**Ab initio** — from the beginning. A contract void ab initio has no legal effect from its inception.

**Actio personalis moritur cum persona** — a personal action dies with the person. Not all personal actions survive death; but in Zimbabwe this principle has been modified by statute.

**Ad hoc** — for this particular purpose; not general.

**Ad idem** — of the same mind. Consensus ad idem is the meeting of minds required for a valid contract.

**Aequitas** — equity; fairness.

**Alibi** — elsewhere; a defence that the accused was somewhere else when the offence occurred.

**Animus** — intention; mental element.

**Animus contrahendi** — the intention to contract; necessary for a valid agreement.

**Animus domini** — the intention to hold as owner; required for possessory interdict.

**Animus iniuriandi** — the intention to injure; the mental element in defamation.

**A priori** — from the prior; reasoning from general principles to particular conclusions.

**Audi alteram partem** — hear the other side. A cardinal principle of natural justice; entrenched in section 68 of the Constitution of Zimbabwe 2013. No person may be prejudiced without being heard.

### B

**Bona fide / Bona fides** — in good faith. The standard of good faith pervades Zimbabwe's common law.

**Bona vacantia** — ownerless property; property without an owner which falls to the State.

### C

**Causa** — cause; reason. The causa of an obligation is the legal ground that justifies the obligation.

**Caveat emptor** — let the buyer beware. The buyer must inspect what he buys. In Zimbabwe, this principle is qualified by the voetstoots clause and implied warranties against latent defects.

**Certiorari** — let it be certified. A prerogative remedy by which the High Court reviews the decisions of inferior courts or tribunals.

**Compensatio** — set-off; the extinction of mutual obligations by operation of law.

**Condictio** — an action for unjustified enrichment.

**Condictio indebiti** — an action to recover money paid by mistake, where no debt existed.

**Consensus ad idem** — a meeting of minds; the agreement of the parties on the essential terms of a contract.

**Contra bonos mores** — against good morals; contrary to public policy. Contracts contra bonos mores are void.

**Corpus** — body; the physical element of possession (physical control of a thing).

### D

**Damnum** — loss; harm.

**Damnum absque injuria** — loss without legal wrong; harm for which the law provides no remedy.

**De bonis propriis** — from one's own goods; a personal cost order against a trustee or attorney who has acted improperly.

**De facto** — in fact; actually existing, as opposed to de jure (by law).

**De jure** — by right; by law; legally recognised.

**Delectus personae** — the selection of a specific person; relevant in contracts where the identity of the party is material.

**Dolus** — fraud; deceit; intention. In delict, the intentional commission of a wrongful act.

**Dolus malus** — fraudulent intention; deceit. Grounds for recission of a contract.

**Duress** — coercion. A contract concluded under duress is voidable.

### E

**Ei incumbit probatio qui dicit, non qui negat** — the burden of proof lies on the one who asserts, not on the one who denies. The onus in civil proceedings.

**Ex aequo et bono** — according to equity and good conscience.

**Ex abundanti cautela** — from an abundance of caution; as a precaution.

**Ex contractu** — arising from contract; as opposed to ex delicto.

**Ex delicto** — arising from a delict (wrongful act).

**Ex parte** — on behalf of one party only; without notice to the other side. Ex parte applications must be followed by service on the respondent.

**Exceptio** — a defence; a plea in bar.

**Exceptio rei judicatae** — the defence of res judicata.

**Exceptio non adimpleti contractus** — the defence of the unfulfilled contract; available in reciprocal contracts where the plaintiff has not performed.

### F

**Factum valet** — the act is valid (notwithstanding that it was irregularly performed).

**Fiat** — let it be done; a court order or authorisation.

**Fides** — faith; trust. *Bona fides* (good faith) is fundamental in Roman-Dutch law.

**Forum** — the court in which proceedings are brought.

**Forum rei sitae** — the court of the place where the thing (especially land) is situated. The court of the situs has jurisdiction in matters relating to immovable property.

### H

**Habeas corpus** — you should have the body. A writ requiring that a detained person be brought before the court to determine the lawfulness of detention.

### I

**Ignorantia juris non excusat** — ignorance of the law is no excuse.

**In curia** — in open court.

**In duplum** — up to double. The duplum rule prevents compound interest from exceeding the capital sum owed.

**In limine** — at the threshold; a preliminary objection raised before the merits are considered.

**In pari delicto** — in equal fault. Where both parties are equally at fault, the position of the defendant is stronger.

**In personam** — against a person; a personal right or action.

**In rem** — against a thing; a real right or action enforceable against the world.

**Inter alia** — among other things.

**Inter partes** — between the parties.

**Interim** — meanwhile; temporary.

**In vacuo** — in a vacuum; in isolation from context.

**Iniuria** — a legal wrong; an intentional infringement of another's rights (personality rights in the Roman-Dutch sense).

### J

**Jus** — right; law.

**Jus cogens** — peremptory norms of international law.

**Jus in personam** — a personal right; enforceable against a specific person.

**Jus in re aliena** — a right in another's property; a limited real right.

**Jus in re propria** — a right in one's own property; ownership.

**Jus in rem** — a real right; enforceable against the world.

### L

**Lacuna** — a gap; an absence of legal provision.

**Laches** — unreasonable delay in asserting a right.

**Lege artis** — in accordance with the standards of the art or profession.

**Lex** — law; statute.

**Lex fori** — the law of the forum (the court hearing the matter).

**Lex loci actus** — the law of the place where the act was performed.

**Lex loci contractus** — the law of the place where the contract was concluded.

**Lex loci solutionis** — the law of the place where the obligation is to be performed.

**Lis pendens** — a pending lawsuit. A special plea that the same matter between the same parties is pending in another court.

**Locus standi** — standing; the right to appear before a court.

**Locus standi in judicio** — the right to institute or defend legal proceedings.

### M

**Mala fide** — in bad faith; fraudulently.

**Mandamus** — we command. A prerogative order commanding a body to perform a public duty.

**Mandamentum van spolie** (Dutch-Latin hybrid) — the spoliation interdict; restores possession unlawfully disturbed without inquiry into the right to possess.

**Meum et tuum** — mine and yours; the distinction between one's own property and another's.

**Mobilia sequuntur personam** — movables follow the person. The law of the domicile governs movable property.

**Mora** — delay; default in performance. Mora debitoris is the debtor's delay.

**Mutatis mutandis** — with the necessary changes having been made.

### N

**Nec spe nec metu** — neither by hope nor by fear. Freedom from inducement required for a valid will.

**Nemo dat quod non habet** — no one gives what one does not have. A seller cannot pass better title than they possess.

**Nemo debet esse judex in propria causa** — no one should be a judge in their own cause. A rule of natural justice.

**Non est factum** — it is not my deed. A defence to enforceability of a document where the signatory was misled as to its nature.

**Novation** — the substitution of one obligation for another, extinguishing the original.

### O

**Onus probandi** — the burden of proof. The onus in civil proceedings lies on the party asserting a positive fact.

**Obligatio** — a legal bond; an obligation.

### P

**Pacta sunt servanda** — agreements must be kept. The foundational principle of contract law.

**Pari passu** — on an equal footing; simultaneously; in proportion.

**Per incuriam** — by inadvertence; a decision made in ignorance of a binding authority.

**Persona standi in judicio** — the capacity to appear in court.

**Plenum dominium** — full ownership.

**Possession animo** — possession with intent; the mental element of possession.

**Possession corpore** — possession by physical control.

**Prima facie** — at first sight; on the face of it. A prima facie case is one which, if unanswered, would succeed.

**Pro se** — for oneself; self-represented in court.

**Prohibition** — a prerogative order preventing an inferior court from exceeding its jurisdiction.

### Q

**Quantum** — the amount; how much. The quantum of damages is the amount of compensation assessed.

**Quantum meruit** — as much as he has deserved. A quasi-contractual remedy for reasonable remuneration.

**Quasi** — as if; resembling. Quasi-contract, quasi-delict.

**Quid pro quo** — something for something; counter-performance.

### R

**Rei vindicatio** — the owner's action to recover property. An owner may claim property from any possessor without right.

**Res** — a thing; property.

**Res extra commercium** — things outside commerce; cannot be privately owned.

**Res judicata** — a matter that has been adjudged. Bars relitigation of the same issue between the same parties.

**Res nullius** — a thing belonging to no one; unowned property which can be acquired by occupation.

**Res perit domino** — the thing perishes for the owner. The owner bears the risk of accidental loss.

### S

**Semble** — it seems; an indication that a legal proposition is probable but not certain.

**Seriatim** — in series; one by one.

**Sic utere tuo ut alienum non laedas** — use your own property so as not to harm another's.

**Sine die** — without a day being fixed; adjourned indefinitely.

**Sine qua non** — without which not; an indispensable condition.

**Situs** — location; the place where property is situated.

**Spoliation** — the unlawful deprivation of possession; gives rise to the mandament van spolie.

**Stare decisis** — to stand by decisions. The doctrine of precedent requires courts to follow prior decisions of superior courts.

**Sub silentio** — in silence; a point not expressly argued or decided.

**Sui juris** — of one's own right; having full legal capacity.

### T

**Tacit** — implied; unspoken. A tacit term is one implied by law.

**Tempore** — at the time of.

**Traditio** — delivery; the transfer of possession constituting transfer of ownership of movable property.

### U

**Uberrimae fidei** — of utmost good faith. Contracts of insurance require utmost good faith from both parties.

**Ultra petita** — beyond what was claimed. A court may not grant more relief than was claimed.

**Ultra vires** — beyond the powers; outside the legal authority.

**Unilateral** — one-sided; proceeding from one party alone.

**Usucaption** — acquisition of ownership by prescription (long possession).

**Usufruct** — the right to use and enjoy another's property without destroying its substance.

### V

**Verbatim** — word for word; exactly as stated.

**Vis major** — superior force; force majeure; an irresistible natural event.

**Voetstoots** — as is; sold with all faults. A clause excluding liability for latent defects. In Zimbabwe, the seller remains liable if the latent defect was fraudulently concealed.

**Volenti non fit injuria** — to a consenting party no injury is done. A defence to delictual liability where the plaintiff consented to the risk.
