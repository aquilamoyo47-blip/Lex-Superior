# SADC Legal Framework and Tribunal Protocol
## Southern African Development Community — Legal Instruments

### Treaty of the Southern African Development Community (Relevant Civil Law Provisions)

**Article 6: Principles**
SADC and its Member States shall act in accordance with the following principles—
(a) sovereign equality of all Member States;
(b) solidarity, peace and security;
(c) human rights, democracy and the rule of law;
(d) equity, balance and mutual benefit;
(e) peaceful settlement of disputes.

**Article 32: Dispute Settlement**
1. Any disputes arising from the interpretation or application of the Treaty, the Protocols and subsidiary instruments of SADC shall be settled through negotiation.
2. If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation, it shall be referred to the Summit which shall make a decision on the dispute.

### Protocol on Tribunal and the Rules of Procedure (Historical)

**Note:** The SADC Tribunal was suspended in 2010 following the *Mike Campbell v Zimbabwe* litigation. This text is retained for historical jurisprudential reference.

**Article 14: Jurisdiction**
The Tribunal shall have jurisdiction over—
(a) disputes between Member States;
(b) disputes between natural or legal persons and Member States;
(c) matters referred to it in accordance with the Treaty and Protocols.

**Article 15: Applicable Law**
The Tribunal shall apply—
(a) the Treaty;
(b) Protocols;
(c) generally accepted principles of public international law;
(d) applicable rules of international law.

### Mutual Legal Assistance in Civil and Commercial Matters

**Purpose:** The SADC Member States have developed frameworks for mutual legal assistance in civil matters, including:

1. **Service of Process:** Member States assist each other in serving judicial documents across borders.
2. **Taking of Evidence:** Courts may request assistance in taking evidence in another Member State.
3. **Recognition of Judgments:** SADC Member States are developing frameworks for the recognition and enforcement of civil judgments.

### Zimbabwe — South Africa Reciprocal Enforcement

Under the Civil Matters (Mutual Assistance) Act [Chapter 8:02], Zimbabwe provides for—
1. Service of process from South African courts in Zimbabwe.
2. Enforcement of South African judgments in Zimbabwe under the Magistrates' Courts Act and High Court Act.
3. Recognition of South African court orders relating to maintenance.

### SADC Model Law on Competition

The SADC region has developed model competition legislation which Zimbabwe has adopted in part through the Competition Act [Chapter 14:28], including provisions relevant to civil litigation arising from anticompetitive practices.
